Unfortunately, I have NOT found very much information on the Hummers.

This is a picture of my Grandmother and Grandfather.  My Grandmothers maiden name was Mabel Anna Hoffer.  She was born 6/26/1891 and died 4/4/1947.  My Grandfather was Harry White Hummer and he was born 2/14/1891 and died on 3/21/1926).  They married on June 29th 1912.  Unfortunately for me I never knew either of my Grandparents, as I was only like 5 months old when my Grandmother died and my Grandfather died like 21 years before I was born.  

  Left to right.. Annabelle (My Mom) Johnny (my uncle Johnny) and Grace (my Aunt Grace)

They had 3 children; 

Annabelle (my Mom) She was born 11/1/1922 and died 8/24/2004.
 Johnny (My Uncle Johnny) He was born 7/8/1924 and died 6/11/1993.
Grace H. (My Aunt Grace) She was born 1/13/1913 and died 1/19/1986.

 I have very little information on my Aunt Grace.  I know she first married Frank Martin and they lived somewhere around Harrisburg.  Frank died and Grace remarried Bucky Taylor and they lived in South Carolina.  Bucky sold Piper airplanes and it was great when we went to visit them as Bucky would always give us airplane rides! :)  Bucky died and Grace again remarried Doc Jones.  I know both Grace and Doc have died.  I don't have any information on Doc.  I believe they lived in Florida.  She was buried in the Manheim Fairview Cemetery.  I'm guessing that her will stated that she be buried in Manheim Fairview Cemetery.

 

My Uncle Johnny was a career Army man and served in WW2, Korea and Vietnam.  His rank was a Major.  His "Find A Grave Memorial#" is 49209270.
He was born July 8th 1924 and died June 11th 1993.
His wife was Jeanne C. (*Cook) she was born June 23rd 1922 and died April 30th 2005.
Jeanne was previously married and had two daughters,  Robin and Phyllis.
Johnny and Jeanne are buried at Arlington Cemetery Plot: Sec: 70, Site: 300
They lived in Alexandria, Va (Fort Belvoir, Va)

Ahhh Haa!  I was talking to my brother Randy (Cos) Dissinger today (1/22/2016) and he said that Jeanne was previously married and her married name was *Cook (not sure of her maiden name, but I think it might have been "Dale" as her daughter Robin's middle name was "Dale") and they had two children, Robin and Phyllis.  They got divorced and later Jeanne married Johnny and Johnny then adopted Robin and Phyllis.

As of 3/22/2016 I found out that Robin Hummer had passed away on 4/9/2007.  She labored with Multiple sclerosis for many years.

Uncle Johnny wrote a book called "An InfantryMan's Journal"

AN INFANTRYMAN'S JOURNAL is a personal account of a life and career that in the course of twenty-four years spanned three wars—World War II, Korea and Vietnam. It is a dramatic story of Hummer's years in the United States Army from his day of enlistment as a Private on November 27th, 1942, to his retirement as a Major on November 30th, 1966. It is the story of an American soldier; how he lived the army life; of what he thought and why; of what he did in and to the Army; and what the Army did to him.

The person in the foreground would of been my my Uncle Johnny (my Grandfather Harry White Hummer's brother). I remember my mother (Annabelle Hummer/Dissinger) telling me about my Uncle Johnny, saying he was mentally challenged but she didn't go into any detail and it was a long time ago, so I don't know if I've remembered everything she might have told me. My half Aunt Darlene Longenecker saying likewise. Per the "Manheim Revisited Compiled & Edited By The Manheim Historical Society" book... "Many Manheim natives will remember Johnny Hummer. He pushed his two-wheeled cart all over town as he gathered papers and junk". The handwriting on the photo is Mom's handwriting stating that the 'Johnny' on the picture was Harry White Hummers brother not her brother 'Johnny'.
