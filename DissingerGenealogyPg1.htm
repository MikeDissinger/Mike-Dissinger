Just an FYI here... in early on times middle names were actually the mother's maiden name.  I.E. John Eberhart Dissinger.  His mother's maiden name would of been Eberhart.

Supposedly John Eberhart Dissinger lived and died in Schaefferstown, PA.  I could not find any record of where he was buried to get a photo of his tombstone. 
No pictures/camera's were in existence at the time John Eberhart Dissinger lived, but I do have the following information on him and his descendants.

John Eberhart Dissinger was born December 14, 1711 (48) in Klarenthal, Saarbrucken, Saarland, Germany.   He was the son of Hans Eberhart Dusinger (would of been my great great great great great grandfather and note the surname spelling "D[U]ssinger") ,  who was born 1660 in Saarbrucken, died 1759.

Hans Eberhart Dusinger was the son of Johann Jacob Dusinger (Johann Jacob Dusinger would of been my great great great great great great grandfather) , born 1640).  Maria Catherine's mother was Anna Catherine Meyer, born 17 March 1717, daughter of John Henry and Sophia Meyer.

John Eberhart Dissinger died 1759 at Schaefferstown, Lebanon, Pennsylvania, United States.   He was (47) years old.

On April 12, 1735 John Eberhart Dissinger marries Anna Gersweiler Dissinger .

John Eberhart Dissinger and Anna Gersweiler Dissinger were immigrants from Germany October 21, 1761.  John Eberhart Dissinger reportedly arrived aboard the ship "Snow Squirrel".  From Rotterdam, Netherland via Cowes, Isle of Wight, carrying immigrants to Pennsylvania.  John Benn was the Captain. They immigrated to Philadelphia, PA

2nd Brigade, King's Division, 22nd Corps, to July, 1863.
